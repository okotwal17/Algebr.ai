What is an Inequality?

An inequality is a mathematical statement that compares two expressions using inequality symbols. The most common symbols are:

  • < (less than)
  • > (greater than)
  • ≤ (less than or equal to)
  • ≥ (greater than or equal to)

Solving Basic Inequalities

To solve inequalities, you follow the same steps as solving equations. However, remember that when you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by a negative number, you must flip the inequality symbol.

Example:

2x - 3 > 5

Solution:

2x > 8
x > 4

Important Rule: If you divide by a negative number, flip the inequality.

Graphing Inequalities on a Number Line

Once you have solved the inequality, you can graph it on a number line. Use an open circle for < and >, and a closed circle for ≤ and ≥. Shade to the left or right to indicate all possible solutions.

Example:

x < 4

Graph:

  • An open circle at 4
  • Shade to the left (towards smaller numbers)

Compound Inequalities

Compound inequalities involve two inequalities connected by the words "and" or "or". An "and" inequality means that both conditions must be true, while an "or" inequality means that at least one condition must be true.

Example (and inequality):

1 < x < 5

Solution: The variable x must be between 1 and 5.

Example (or inequality):

x < -2 or x > 3

Solution: The variable x can be less than -2 or greater than 3.

Solving Absolute Value Inequalities

An absolute value inequality involves finding the values of a variable that satisfy the inequality, taking into account that absolute value represents the distance from zero.

Example:

|x - 2| < 3

Solution: This becomes two inequalities:

-3 < x - 2 < 3

Now solve for x:

-1 < x < 5
