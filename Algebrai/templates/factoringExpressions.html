What is Factoring?

Factoring is the process of breaking down an expression into a product of simpler expressions, or factors. It’s an essential skill when solving quadratic equations or simplifying algebraic expressions.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

Factoring out the Greatest Common Factor (GCF) is the first step in many factoring problems. The GCF is the largest expression that divides all terms in the polynomial.

Example:

6x² + 12x = 6x(x + 2)

Factoring Trinomials

For trinomials of the form ax² + bx + c, factoring involves finding two numbers that multiply to give ac and add to give b.

Example:

x² + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3)

Here, 2 and 3 multiply to 6 and add to 5.

Factoring the Difference of Squares

A difference of squares is factored as:

a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)

Example:

x² - 16 = (x + 4)(x - 4)
